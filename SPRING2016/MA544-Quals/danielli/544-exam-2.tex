\begin{problem}
Assume that $f\in L^1(\bbR^n)$. Show that for every $\varepsilon>0$ there
exists a ball $B$, centered at the origin, such that
\[
\int_{\bbR^n\minus B}|f|<\varepsilon.
\]
\end{problem}
\begin{proof}
Recall that $f\in L^1(\bbR^n)$ if and only if $|f|\in L^1(\bbR^n)$. Since
$|f|$ is nonnegative, $|f|\in L^1(\bbR^n)$ implies that $|f|$ is finite
a.e.\@ on $\bbR^n$. Pick some sufficiently large real number $M$ such that
$M>|f|$ a.e.\@ on $\bbR^n$.
\end{proof}
